What I do

Claim → assumptions → inference
  • Clarify what a claim actually asserts (and what it doesn’t).
  • Surface key assumptions—explicit and implicit.
  • Stress-test evidence → inference alignment.
  • Identify plausible failure modes and decision-relevant uncertainties.

The goal is not certainty, but clarity under scrutiny.

This is
  • Structured, written second-opinion review
  • Explicit about limits and uncertainty
  • Readable by technical and non-technical stakeholders
  • Documentation you can retain as a record
This is not
  • Business/market/investment advice
  • Endorsement or advocacy
  • Ghostwriting or promotional copy
  • A substitute for running studies/experiments

When this is useful

Decision support
  • Pre-funding or pre-investment technical review
  • Internal validation of research-driven projects
  • Grant or proposal vetting
  • Policy- or compliance-relevant analysis
  • Litigation-adjacent or expert-style methodological review

In many cases, the value is identifying weaknesses before they become public, costly, or reputationally damaging.

Deliverables

Tiered depth

Clients receive a written review memo prepared for both technical and non-technical readers. Reviews explicitly separate facts, assumptions, evidence, and inference.

Executive Review (3–5 pages) High-level assessment of claim clarity, core assumptions, major risks, and decision-relevant uncertainties.
Standard Review (8–12 pages) Detailed analysis of assumptions, methods, inference, and failure modes for internal diligence or advisory use.
Comprehensive Review (15–20 pages) Full methodological stress-testing and documentation for legal, regulatory, or high-stakes institutional contexts.

If desired, I can also provide a one-page Decision Brief distilled from the memo for fast executive review.

Engagement scope and pricing are determined after initial review of materials. Reviews are fixed-fee and scaled to complexity, not hourly.
